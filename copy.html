BCSIR School and College

History

This school started its journey on 22nd January 1968 with 40 children in the grounds of Bangladesh Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (BCSIR) under the initiative of BCSIR Ladies Club. It was registered as a primary school in 1980. It was recognized as a high school in 1984, and the school has grown in fame and glory. Today, it has a three-storey and a two-storey teaching building, along with an administrative building.

After serving as the headmaster of the school from March 1, 1969, Priyadarshini Mrs. Jebunnesa Begum voluntarily left the post of headmaster and served as assistant headmaster until her retirement. Mr. Kazi Nurul Haque joined as headmaster in 1983. In 1986, 11 students participated in the 1st SSC examination, and all of them passed with distinction. At present, the school operates science, humanities, and business education branches. It features a rich science laboratory, a computer lab, and a separate library.

Code of Conduct

  • All students must attend school regularly.
  • Students must wear school uniforms.
  • School badge and prescribed shoes must be worn regularly.
  • All students must come to school with required books and routines.
  • Students' hair must be cut moderately short. Girls must tie their hair in two braids with white ribbons or bands.
  • Girls are not allowed to wear chains, large earrings, or bangles. However, small rings or tops are permitted.
  • No henna, tattoos, or nail polish are allowed.
  • Students are prohibited from bringing mobile phones to school. If found, they will be confiscated.
  • Students absent for 75% of the total working days will not be allowed to take exams and will be given a "C".
